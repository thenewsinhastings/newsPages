A new graphic novel is captivating the attention of readers and history enthusiasts alike, bringing to life one of the most significant events in British history: the Battle of Hastings. Published by a local independent press, the book offers an engaging visual narrative that captures the dramatic events of October 14, 1066, when William the Conqueror defeated Harold Godwinson near Hastings.

The graphic novel features detailed illustrations and meticulous research, making it an invaluable educational tool for both children and adults. It is already being used in local schools to help students understand this pivotal moment in English history more vividly than traditional textbooks can provide.

Local historians and enthusiasts are celebrating the release of the book as a way to reconnect Hastings residents with their rich historical roots, while also attracting tourists interested in Anglo-Saxon and Norman heritage. With its blend of historical accuracy and artistic flair, the novel promises to be a valuable addition to the local cultural scene.
