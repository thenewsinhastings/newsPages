Local authorities in Hastings are investigating the illegal dumping of cannabis waste in Brede High Woods, raising serious concerns about the impact on local wildlife and the environment. The estimated four tonnes of plant material were discovered at a secluded site within the woods, posing immediate risks to the habitat's flora and fauna.

The incident has sparked outrage among residents who are calling for stricter enforcement against illegal cannabis growing operations that exploit natural areas. Environmental groups warn of long-term damage due to the high levels of nutrients and pesticides present in the discarded plants.

Detectives from the East Sussex Police have begun an investigation into the source of this waste, seeking information from the public about any recent suspicious activity near Brede High Woods. The case highlights the ongoing challenge faced by local law enforcement in managing environmental impacts associated with illegal drug production.
