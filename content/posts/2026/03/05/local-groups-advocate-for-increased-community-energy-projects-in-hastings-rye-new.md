In a joint statement released this week, several local groups in Hastings and Rye have called for greater support from the government to unlock community energy projects on a larger scale. The initiative aims to enhance renewable energy production and distribution within the region by encouraging more investment into local renewable projects.

According to the group's spokesperson, unlocking community energy at scale would provide significant benefits to both residents and businesses in Hastings and Rye. Not only could it lead to lower energy costs for consumers but also reduce environmental impacts and foster economic growth through job creation.

The groups emphasize that while there has been some progress in recent years towards decentralizing the UK’s power grid, more needs to be done to make such projects accessible to all communities across East Sussex. They urge local authorities and national policymakers alike to take action now if they wish to see tangible results from these initiatives.
