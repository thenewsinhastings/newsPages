In a bid to boost local energy production and sustainability efforts, various community groups in Hastings and Rye have joined forces to advocate for the 'unlocking' of community energy projects on a larger scale. These initiatives aim to empower residents by enabling them to participate actively in renewable energy generation and distribution.

The coalition of environmental activists, housing associations, and local businesses has called upon regional authorities to remove barriers that hinder the development of such schemes. They argue that widespread adoption of community-owned solar panels, wind turbines, and other green technologies would not only reduce carbon footprints but also enhance economic resilience within the area.

'Community energy is a vital component in our journey towards net-zero emissions,' said Jane Thompson, spokesperson for Green Horizons Coalition. 'By supporting these ventures, we can create jobs, improve air quality, and foster a sense of community ownership over local resources.'

Further details on specific proposals will be presented at an upcoming public meeting scheduled for next month in the Hastings Town Hall, inviting all interested parties to contribute their ideas and support.
