In a bid to enhance community energy efforts on both local and national levels, several groups from Hastings and the surrounding area have come together to urge for greater access to renewable energy projects. The initiative aims at unlocking the potential of community-led renewable energy schemes by fostering collaboration between various stakeholders such as residents, businesses, and local authorities.

The call comes in response to growing concerns over climate change and sustainability within the region. Local leaders believe that community energy projects can play a significant role in reducing carbon footprints while promoting economic growth through job creation and reduced energy costs for residents.

Representatives from various organisations including the Hastings Community Energy Group, Rye Renewables Association, and the Sussex Sustainable Living Network have joined forces to push forward this agenda. They are seeking support from local councils and government bodies to facilitate easier access to funding and regulatory approval processes necessary for launching such initiatives.

The collaboration seeks not only to empower communities but also to foster innovation in renewable energy technology. By working together, these groups hope to set an example that can inspire similar projects across the UK.
