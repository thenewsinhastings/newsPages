In a move aimed at fostering greater community engagement with renewable energy, several local groups have joined forces to advocate for the 'unlocking of community energy' in Hastings and Rye. The initiative seeks to empower residents by providing them with more control over their energy sources through cooperative ownership models.

Representatives from various organizations highlighted that this collaborative approach would not only help reduce carbon emissions but also strengthen local economies by creating jobs and reducing reliance on national grid infrastructure. Participants noted the importance of such grassroots efforts in shaping a sustainable future for both towns, where the transition to renewable energy has been gaining traction among residents.

The campaign is currently gathering support from businesses, households, and community leaders who are encouraged to sign a petition calling on local authorities to facilitate the process by offering regulatory assistance and incentives. The groups hope their collective voice will resonate with decision-makers, leading to tangible changes in policy that benefit everyone involved.
