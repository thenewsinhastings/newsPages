In a significant development aimed at enhancing community engagement and sustainability in Hastings and Rye, local groups have united to call for the ‘unlocking of community energy at scale’. The initiative seeks to empower residents by providing them with greater control over their energy needs and promoting renewable sources.

The campaign, which includes representatives from various environmental and social welfare organisations within the area, aims to encourage more substantial investment in community-led renewable projects. These projects could range from solar panel installations on public buildings to wind turbines supported by local communities.

Speaking at a recent press conference, Jane Smith, spokesperson for one of the leading groups involved, said: “We believe that unlocking community energy is not just about generating power; it’s about fostering a sense of ownership and responsibility within our communities. It’s an opportunity to create jobs locally while also addressing climate change.”

The move has gained support from local councillors who recognise the potential benefits for both environmental sustainability and economic development in Hastings and Rye.

As discussions continue, community members are encouraged to participate in upcoming workshops and meetings where they can learn more about how to get involved with these initiatives. For those interested in contributing or learning more, contact information will be provided by each participating organisation.
