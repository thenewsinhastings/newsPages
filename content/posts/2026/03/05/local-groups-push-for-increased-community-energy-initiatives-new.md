Several local groups in Hastings and Rye are calling for the 'unlocking of community energy at scale' to promote sustainability and self-sufficiency within the region. The initiative aims to empower residents with renewable energy options, reduce carbon footprints, and foster a sense of community ownership over environmental projects.

Supporters argue that community-led energy solutions can help local areas become more resilient in the face of climate change, while also creating jobs and enhancing the quality of life for residents. Advocates are urging both Hastings Borough Council and Rye Town Council to take proactive measures to support these efforts through policy changes and financial incentives.

The campaign has garnered widespread community support, with numerous local organisations signing up to lend their backing. It is hoped that this collective effort will inspire other communities across the country to explore similar initiatives.
