HASTINGS AND RYE - A coalition of local groups has joined forces to urge for greater support and investment in community energy projects, aiming to unlock a more sustainable future for Hastings and Rye.

Representatives from several organisations met this week to discuss the importance of scaling up community-led renewable energy initiatives. The group advocates for improved access to funding and resources to enable local communities to lead the way in reducing carbon emissions and enhancing energy security.

The initiative follows recent successes by smaller groups within Hastings and Rye, who have already installed solar panels on public buildings and established energy-saving schemes that benefit residents directly.

"It’s crucial that we support community-led efforts as they offer a practical pathway towards achieving our environmental goals," commented Jane Smith, spokesperson for the local campaign. "By fostering collaboration between communities, businesses, and local authorities, we can ensure a more sustainable future for Hastings and Rye.”

The group will be presenting their proposals to local councillors next month with the hope of securing commitments from decision-makers to support such initiatives on a larger scale.
