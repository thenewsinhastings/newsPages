In a bid to promote sustainable living and community empowerment, several local groups in Hastings and Rye have come together to advocate for the 'unlocking of community energy at scale.' The initiative aims to facilitate greater access to renewable energy resources for households and businesses within the region.

Organisations such as Greenpeace Hastings, Transition Town Rye, and the Coastal Energy Alliance are leading the charge by organising public discussions and workshops. These events aim to educate residents on the benefits of community-led renewable projects, which could include solar panels, wind turbines, and district heating systems.

The groups argue that these initiatives not only reduce carbon emissions but also empower local communities financially through ownership and control over energy production. Furthermore, they highlight the potential for job creation in the green economy sector within Hastings and Rye.

Representatives from the Coastal Energy Alliance stated, 'By unlocking community energy at scale, we can help tackle climate change while boosting our local economies. This is a chance to make a real difference on both an environmental and socio-economic level.'

Local authorities have been encouraged to support these grassroots movements by providing regulatory frameworks that facilitate the development of such projects. As more communities across East Sussex consider similar initiatives, the collaboration between groups in Hastings and Rye could set a precedent for wider adoption throughout the region.
