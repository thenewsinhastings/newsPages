In a joint effort to promote sustainable energy solutions within the Hastings and Rye area, several local groups have united in their call for government support to ‘unlock community energy at scale.’ The initiative seeks to empower residents and small communities by providing them with greater access to renewable energy sources.

Representatives from the Hastings Energy Co-operative, Transition Town Hastings, and the Rye Community Council met this week to discuss strategies aimed at increasing public engagement in local green projects. They emphasize that these efforts are not only beneficial for the environment but also crucial for reducing reliance on fossil fuels and fostering economic growth within the community.

During a press conference held at the town hall, leaders of these organizations highlighted the need for legislative changes to simplify regulations surrounding renewable energy investments. This would allow more people to participate in projects that contribute positively to both environmental sustainability and local employment opportunities.

The groups also expressed their intention to collaborate with educational institutions and businesses within Hastings and Rye to develop a robust framework supporting community-led initiatives. They are optimistic about the potential for such collaborations to drive innovation, enhance local resilience against climate change, and create new job prospects in the renewable energy sector.
