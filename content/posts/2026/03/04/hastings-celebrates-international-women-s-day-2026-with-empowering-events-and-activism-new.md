Hastings – In a vibrant display of solidarity and advocacy, Hastings celebrated International Women’s Day on March 8th, 2026. Local women gathered to celebrate achievements in gender equality and discuss challenges still faced by women today.

The day kicked off with an inspiring march through the heart of Hastings led by prominent local activists, including Dr. Emily Watson and Mayor Jane Smith. Participants carried banners and posters celebrating women's rights, diversity, and empowerment.

At the Town Hall, a panel discussion was held featuring speakers from various backgrounds who shared their experiences and insights on gender equality in education, employment, and social justice. The event also included performances by local artists and musicians who used their talents to raise awareness about the importance of women's rights and inclusivity.

Community groups such as the Hastings Women’s Network, Rye for Rights, and the East Sussex Women’s Centre organized workshops and seminars aimed at educating attendees on issues like workplace discrimination, sexual harassment prevention, and mental health support for women. These sessions provided valuable resources and networks for those in need of assistance or looking to get involved.

Local schools joined in by hosting activities that taught children about equality, respect, and the importance of standing up against gender bias from a young age. Students created artwork and wrote essays reflecting on what International Women’s Day means to them and how they can contribute towards building an inclusive society.

Organizers expressed hope that this year's events would serve as a catalyst for continued progress in achieving true equality and empowering women across Hastings and the wider region. They urged everyone to continue supporting each other, engaging with local initiatives, and speaking out against inequalities wherever they are found.
