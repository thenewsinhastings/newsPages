In a collective effort to enhance local energy sustainability and independence, several community groups from Hastings and Rye have issued an appeal for the 'unlocking of community energy at scale'. This initiative aims to harness renewable resources within the region and empower communities with more control over their energy supply.

The call has been echoed by environmental advocates and local residents alike who are concerned about the rising costs and environmental impacts associated with traditional energy sources. These groups believe that investing in large-scale, community-driven renewable projects will not only provide economic benefits but also foster a sense of unity and shared responsibility among locals.

Among those leading the charge is the Hastings Community Energy Group (HCEG), who have already begun engaging with stakeholders to explore viable options for solar farms, wind turbines, and other clean energy solutions. The group's spokesperson, Jane Doe, emphasized that 'the time has come for us to take our future into our own hands'.

The initiative also seeks to address the growing demand for affordable housing by integrating renewable energy systems with sustainable building practices in new developments.

As part of their campaign, community leaders plan to host public meetings and forums to gather input from residents about what kind of energy solutions would best serve the local population. They are calling on both businesses and individuals within Hastings and Rye to join them in this vital mission for a greener future.
