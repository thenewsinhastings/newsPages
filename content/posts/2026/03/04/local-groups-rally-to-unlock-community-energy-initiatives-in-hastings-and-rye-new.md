In a significant push towards sustainable living, local groups in Hastings and Rye have joined forces to advocate for the 'unlocking of community energy at scale'. This initiative aims to enhance the use of renewable energy sources within the region by empowering communities with tools and resources necessary for large-scale energy projects.

Representatives from various environmental groups and community organizations gathered today to discuss strategies that would enable local residents to contribute more actively towards reducing carbon emissions. The event, which was held at Rye Town Hall, saw passionate speeches about the importance of collective action in tackling climate change and promoting eco-friendly practices within our communities.

Participants highlighted the need for better access to information and funding opportunities specifically tailored for grassroots energy initiatives. They also called upon local authorities to streamline regulations that currently hinder community-led projects from reaching their full potential.

“We are excited about this initiative,” said Jane Thompson, spokesperson for Hastings Green Energy Group. “By working together and pooling our resources, we can make a real difference in how our communities generate and consume energy.”

The gathering concluded with the announcement of upcoming workshops designed to educate interested individuals on practical steps they can take towards implementing community-based renewable energy solutions.

For more information or to get involved, visit www.hastingsgreenenergy.org.
