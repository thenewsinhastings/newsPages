In a significant push towards renewable energy solutions within the community, several local groups have rallied together to urge for an expansion of community-led initiatives aimed at increasing access to sustainable power sources.

The coalition includes environmental organisations, business networks, and residents’ associations from both Hastings and Rye. The call comes as part of a broader national movement encouraging greater decentralisation of energy production and distribution.

"It's crucial that we harness the collective strength of our community to address pressing environmental challenges," said Emily Green, chairperson of Hastings Environmental Action (HEA). "Local solutions can be far more effective in meeting local needs."

The groups are now seeking support from local authorities to facilitate easier access to grants and funding opportunities for community energy projects. They aim to showcase the benefits of such schemes through a series of public meetings scheduled across both towns over the coming weeks.

"We want Hastings and Rye residents to understand that they can be part of creating cleaner, greener solutions within their own neighbourhoods," added Tom Robinson from the Hastings Business Network (HBN).

The initiative is seen as an important step towards achieving regional sustainability goals while also fostering a sense of local pride and responsibility. With public interest growing in environmental issues, these groups are optimistic about the potential impact of grassroots efforts on the region’s energy landscape.
