HASTINGS, England – In a glittering showcase of local musical talent, 14-year-old Ryan Zhu triumphed in the Hastings Symphony Orchestra’s annual piano concerto competition. The event, held at the historic St. Leonards Town Hall last Saturday evening, saw an array of young pianists from across the region vie for the prestigious title and a cash prize.

Ryan, a student at Rye College, demonstrated exceptional skill and artistry in his performance of Beethoven’s “Emperor Concerto.” His flawless technique and emotional depth captivated the audience and the judges alike. The competition, now in its 15th year, aims to nurture musical talent within the community and has become a highlight in Hastings’ cultural calendar.

The adjudicator, renowned pianist Dr. Susan Hartley, praised Ryan’s interpretation of Beethoven’s masterpiece, noting his ability to convey both the stormy brilliance and tender intimacy required for such a challenging piece. Other finalists included Mia Johnson from Bexhill-on-Sea and Leo Thompson from Rye.

Congratulations also go to all those who participated in this year’s competition. The event not only celebrates musical excellence but also fosters community spirit, bringing together music lovers of all ages.
