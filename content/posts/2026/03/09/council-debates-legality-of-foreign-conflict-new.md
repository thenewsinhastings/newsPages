In a heated discussion at the Hastings Borough Council meeting yesterday evening, councillors debated the implications of recent international conflicts and their impact on local governance.

The debate was triggered by concerns raised by several councillors regarding the legality of foreign wars from a domestic perspective. They argued that ongoing debates about international affairs could influence public opinion and community relations within Hastings and Rye.

Council members emphasized the importance of focusing on local issues while acknowledging the broader political context. Mayor Jane Roberts stated, 'While we respect national policies, our primary duty is to address the needs of our residents.'

The council also discussed potential measures to support veterans and their families in the region, highlighting the community's commitment to those affected by international conflicts.

This meeting underscores the delicate balance between local governance and national political events that impact communities locally.
