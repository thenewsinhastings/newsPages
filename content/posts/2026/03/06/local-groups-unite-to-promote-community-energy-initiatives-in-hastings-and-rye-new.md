In a collaborative effort aimed at fostering community-led energy initiatives, several local groups in Hastings and Rye have called for the 'unlocking of community energy at scale.' This move is seen as crucial for both environmental sustainability and economic growth within the region.

Organisations such as Renewables East Sussex and Transition Town Hastings are leading the charge. They believe that by harnessing community power, residents can benefit from more affordable and sustainable energy solutions while also contributing to national carbon reduction targets.

The initiative aims to encourage local communities to invest in renewable technologies like solar panels, wind turbines, and battery storage systems. Advocates argue that such projects not only reduce reliance on fossil fuels but also create employment opportunities within the community.

Councillor John Thompson of Hastings Borough Council has expressed support for these efforts, stating: 'We are committed to supporting local initiatives that promote sustainability and empower our communities. These energy projects align perfectly with our borough's vision for a greener future.'

Further details about how residents can get involved or contribute ideas will be shared in the coming weeks through local community meetings and online platforms.
