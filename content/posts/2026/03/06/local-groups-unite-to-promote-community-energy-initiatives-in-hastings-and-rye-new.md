Several local community groups have come together to advocate for a significant expansion of community energy projects across Hastings and Rye. The initiative aims to 'unlock community energy at scale' by encouraging residents and businesses to participate in renewable energy schemes.

The coalition of groups includes environmental activists, local councillors, and representatives from the business sector who believe that such initiatives can not only reduce carbon emissions but also provide financial benefits for local communities through reduced energy costs and job creation opportunities. They argue that a collective approach is necessary to make substantial progress towards sustainable energy goals.

Proposed actions include setting up solar panels in public spaces, encouraging home insulation improvements, and promoting the installation of renewable heating systems like heat pumps. The groups are also calling for stronger support from local authorities to facilitate these initiatives.

This collaboration between various stakeholders highlights a growing community effort to tackle climate change locally while fostering economic development within Hastings and Rye.
