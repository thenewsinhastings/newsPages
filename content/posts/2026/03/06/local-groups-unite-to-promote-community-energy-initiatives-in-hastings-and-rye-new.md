In a recent gathering at the Town Hall Square in Hastings, local community groups have come together to advocate for increased support towards 'community energy,' aiming to unlock its potential on a larger scale. The event brought together representatives from various organizations including Rye Community Association and Hastings Environmental Alliance.

Participants emphasized the importance of collective action in addressing environmental challenges while fostering economic resilience within the region. They discussed initiatives such as community-owned renewable projects, energy efficiency programs for local businesses, and educational workshops to raise awareness about sustainable living practices among residents.

The meeting concluded with a call to action directed at local councillors and other stakeholders urging them to prioritize policies that facilitate the growth of community-led energy schemes. Speakers stressed that by empowering communities through such initiatives, Hastings and Rye can set an example for other towns in tackling climate change while enhancing social cohesion and economic benefits.
