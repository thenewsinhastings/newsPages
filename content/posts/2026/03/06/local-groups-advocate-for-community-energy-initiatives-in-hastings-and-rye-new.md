In a move to enhance local energy independence and sustainability, several community groups in Hastings and Rye have joined forces to advocate for the establishment of large-scale community energy projects. These initiatives aim to unlock significant benefits for residents by promoting renewable energy sources and fostering a more resilient local economy.

The coalition, comprising representatives from environmental charities, local businesses, and civic societies, is calling on government agencies and regional councils to support their efforts in developing these projects. The groups believe that such measures will not only reduce carbon emissions but also provide economic opportunities for the community through job creation and cost savings on energy bills.

“We are seeing a growing recognition of the importance of local energy solutions,” commented Jane Smith, a spokesperson for one of the participating organisations. “By working together to unlock community energy at scale, we can make a substantial impact on both environmental and economic fronts.”

The initiative has garnered support from various quarters within Hastings and Rye, with many residents expressing enthusiasm about the potential benefits these projects could bring.

As discussions continue, local groups are optimistic that their advocacy will lead to tangible outcomes that contribute positively to the region’s future.
