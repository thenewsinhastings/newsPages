In a push for greater community engagement in energy initiatives, several local groups have joined forces to advocate for the expansion of community-led renewable energy projects throughout Hastings and Rye.

Representatives from various organisations met at the Town Hall last week to discuss strategies that would enable residents to harness their collective power towards sustainable energy solutions on a larger scale. The meeting was part of an ongoing effort to reduce carbon emissions and promote local resilience in the face of climate change.

Attendees agreed on the importance of collaboration between businesses, civic leaders, and citizens alike to unlock opportunities for community-based renewable projects that can provide both economic benefits and environmental advantages. Plans are already underway for pilot schemes in selected areas within Hastings and Rye, with more ambitious goals set for future implementations across the region.

As this initiative gains momentum, local activists hope it will inspire similar efforts nationwide, demonstrating how grassroots action can drive significant change towards a greener tomorrow.
