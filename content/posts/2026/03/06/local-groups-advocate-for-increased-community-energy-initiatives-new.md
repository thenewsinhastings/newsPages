A number of local groups in Hastings and Rye are urging greater support for community energy projects to boost sustainability and reduce carbon footprints within the region. The call comes as part of a growing movement aiming to empower communities by providing them with the tools necessary to generate their own renewable energy.

Organisers hope that such initiatives will not only help in tackling climate change but also provide economic benefits through job creation and cost savings for residents involved in these projects. With the local council looking into various options, community leaders are pushing hard to ensure that any new policies or funding allocations take into account the potential of small-scale, grassroots energy solutions.

Residents interested in contributing towards this cause can attend an upcoming meeting where representatives from participating groups will be available to discuss plans and answer questions.
