In a significant move towards sustainable energy solutions, local community groups in Hastings and Rye have come together to advocate for the expansion of community-led renewable energy projects. This initiative aims to unlock larger-scale community energy schemes that can provide long-term benefits to both towns.

The coalition of environmental advocates, residents' associations, and local businesses is calling on policymakers to support greater decentralisation of energy production within the region. They argue that such a shift would not only reduce carbon emissions but also strengthen local economies by creating jobs in green sectors.

"We believe that community-led initiatives can play a vital role in the transition towards renewable energies," said Emma Jones, coordinator for the Hastings Community Energy Group. "By empowering residents to take ownership of their energy production, we can foster innovation and resilience within our communities." 

The groups are also advocating for better access to funding, regulatory support, and increased public awareness about the benefits of community-owned renewables. They hope that through collaborative efforts with local authorities, they can pave the way for a more sustainable future in Hastings and Rye.

Residents interested in getting involved or learning more about these initiatives are encouraged to attend upcoming meetings scheduled at various locations across both towns.
