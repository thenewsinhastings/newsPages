Local communities in Hastings and Rye are stepping up their efforts to promote sustainable energy solutions. Various groups have united to advocate for the 'unlocking of community energy at scale', aiming to address local environmental concerns while fostering economic development.

The initiative seeks to empower residents through grassroots renewable projects, such as solar panels on communal buildings and wind turbines in open spaces. Advocates believe that these small-scale but significant steps could contribute greatly to regional sustainability goals.

Representatives from the Hastings Climate Action Group and Rye Community Energy Forum highlighted the importance of community-led initiatives in tackling climate change at a recent public meeting. They called for greater support from local authorities to facilitate such projects, emphasizing the potential benefits both environmentally and economically.

'Community energy can play a pivotal role in our transition towards cleaner, greener living,' said Jane Thompson, chairperson of Hastings Climate Action Group. 'By involving all members of society, we aim to create tangible change that resonates within our communities.'

The movement has received support from local businesses and residents alike, with many expressing interest in getting involved or volunteering their skills.

Further information on how to join the campaign and participate in upcoming events will be made available through local community centres and online platforms.
