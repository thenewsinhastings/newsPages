In a growing effort to address local energy challenges, several community groups in Hastings and Rye have joined forces to call on the government and businesses to support initiatives that would 'unlock community energy at scale.' This collective push comes as part of ongoing discussions about renewable energy sources and sustainable living practices within the region.

Representatives from various organizations met recently to discuss potential strategies for increasing local energy production through community-led projects. These initiatives aim not only to reduce reliance on non-renewable energy but also to provide economic benefits and environmental sustainability for residents in Hastings and Rye.

The groups are hoping that by collaborating, they can raise awareness about the importance of community-driven solutions in tackling national and global energy issues. This includes encouraging local businesses and individuals to participate actively in projects like solar panel installations on communal buildings and wind turbine investments.

'Community-led energy solutions offer a significant opportunity for us to take control over our power supply while also contributing positively towards climate change goals,' commented Laura Smith, an advocate from one of the participating groups. The group is calling for increased support from both local authorities and national policies that encourage such community-based endeavors.
