In a significant move towards sustainable energy solutions, local groups in Hastings and Rye have thrown their support behind the 'Unlocking Community Energy at Scale' campaign. The initiative aims to empower communities by providing them with greater control over renewable energy projects.

The campaign, spearheaded by various grassroots organisations, seeks to promote community-owned solar panels, wind farms, and other renewable sources of power generation within the region. Advocates believe that such initiatives not only contribute to reducing carbon footprints but also offer substantial economic benefits for local residents through job creation and financial incentives.

"This is a crucial step towards building a greener future for our communities," said Jane Thompson, spokesperson for one of the supporting groups. "By harnessing local resources, we can ensure that the transition to renewable energy is fair and inclusive." 

The movement has already garnered widespread support among residents who are eager to see more local involvement in major projects affecting their area. Local leaders have welcomed the call, with Mayor James Smith expressing hope that such efforts will help Hastings and Rye lead the way in sustainable development.

As discussions continue on how best to implement these ideas, the community looks forward to a future where energy generation is as much about local empowerment as it is about environmental responsibility.
