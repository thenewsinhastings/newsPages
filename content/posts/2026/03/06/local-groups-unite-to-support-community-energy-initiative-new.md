HASTINGS & RYE - A coalition of local groups has thrown their support behind a campaign aimed at 'unlocking community energy at scale' across Hastings and Rye. The initiative seeks to empower communities by facilitating the development of renewable energy projects that are locally owned and managed.

Organizers believe this approach will not only reduce carbon emissions but also foster economic growth through job creation and lower energy costs for residents. Among those endorsing the campaign is the Hastings Energy Group, alongside several environmental NGOs and local businesses.

The project aims to engage with the community to identify suitable sites for renewable energy installations such as solar farms or wind turbines. It will also provide training and support for those interested in participating actively in these ventures.

‘This is a unique opportunity for our communities to take control of their future by investing in sustainable technologies,’ said John Roberts, spokesperson for Hastings Energy Group. ‘We’re excited about the potential for real impact and change.’

More information on how local residents can get involved will be made available at upcoming public meetings scheduled across Hastings and Rye.
