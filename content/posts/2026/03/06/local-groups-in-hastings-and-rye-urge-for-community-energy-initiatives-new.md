A coalition of local groups in Hastings and Rye is calling on the community to come together to unlock energy projects at a larger scale. The initiative, aimed at promoting renewable energy sources within the region, seeks to empower residents by providing them with more control over their energy production.

The collaboration includes environmental advocates, local businesses, and concerned citizens who believe that such initiatives are essential for both economic development and sustainable living. They argue that community-led projects can lead to significant reductions in carbon emissions while also creating jobs and stimulating local economies.

‘We’re excited about the potential of community energy,’ said Sarah Green from Rye Environment Group. ‘This isn’t just about installing solar panels; it’s about transforming how we use and think about energy.’

The groups are inviting all members of the public to join them in their efforts, whether through volunteering, financial support, or simply spreading awareness. They aim to foster a sense of collective responsibility towards addressing climate change while improving quality of life for residents.

For more information on how you can get involved with these community energy projects, visit [local group website] or contact your local representatives.
