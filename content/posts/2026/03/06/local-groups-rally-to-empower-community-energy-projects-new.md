Residents and local organisations in Hastings and Rye are rallying behind a campaign aimed at unlocking community energy initiatives on a larger scale. The initiative, which has been gaining traction in recent months, seeks to harness the collective power of the community to develop sustainable and renewable energy projects that can benefit both individuals and businesses within the region.

According to Sarah Jenkins, Chief Reporter for our newspaper, the campaign is designed to provide local communities with a platform to voice their support for initiatives that promote cleaner energy solutions. By working together, it’s hoped that residents will have greater influence in shaping future projects that align with environmental goals while also supporting local economic growth.

A number of community groups and individuals are joining forces to push for policy changes at both the local council level and regional government offices. Their aim is to reduce barriers currently faced by small-scale renewable energy schemes, making it easier for communities to invest in solar panels, wind turbines, or other forms of green technology.
