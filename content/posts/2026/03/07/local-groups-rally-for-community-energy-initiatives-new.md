Local groups in Hastings and Rye are uniting to advocate for increased community energy initiatives aimed at promoting local renewable energy projects. The campaign, known as the 'Unlock Community Energy' movement, seeks to empower residents by providing them with more control over their own energy production and consumption.

Speaking on behalf of several participating organisations, Jane Doe, a spokesperson from Hastings Renewable Energy Group (HREG), highlighted the importance of community-driven solutions in tackling local environmental challenges. She emphasised that such initiatives not only reduce carbon footprints but also bolster economic resilience within the region by creating new jobs and stimulating investment.

The movement has garnered support across various sectors, including educational institutions, businesses, and household representatives who see it as a vital step towards achieving sustainability goals at both individual and collective levels. Upcoming events include workshops and public discussions aimed at informing residents about available opportunities and encouraging active participation in these projects.
