In a push to promote local sustainability and energy efficiency, several community groups in Hastings & Rye have called on the regional council to support initiatives aimed at 'unlocking community energy at scale'. The move comes as part of an ongoing campaign to encourage residents to take charge of their renewable energy needs through collective action.

Representatives from local organisations emphasised the importance of harnessing wind and solar power within the community, suggesting that such measures would not only reduce carbon footprints but also bring economic benefits by creating jobs in the renewable sector. The groups are urging the council to facilitate this transition by providing necessary support and infrastructure for community-led projects.

Speaking at a press conference, Jane Smith from the Hastings Energy Co-operative highlighted the potential impact of these initiatives on both local communities and the environment. 'We believe that by working together, we can achieve significant energy savings while fostering a more sustainable future,' she said.

The council has yet to make an official statement regarding this proposal but community advocates remain optimistic about the possibility of seeing such projects come to fruition in Hastings & Rye.
