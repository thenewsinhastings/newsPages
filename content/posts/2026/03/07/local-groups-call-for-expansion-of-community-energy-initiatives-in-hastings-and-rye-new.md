Residents and local groups in Hastings and Rye are rallying behind a campaign to expand community-led energy projects across the region. The initiative aims to 'unlock community energy at scale,' enabling more individuals and organisations within the area to take part in sustainable energy solutions.

The call for action comes as many communities seek to reduce carbon footprints and address climate change through practical, local measures. Local leaders are now considering how best to support these grassroots efforts with resources and policy changes that could make a significant impact on both environmental and economic fronts.

In recent weeks, several groups have organised events aimed at raising awareness of the potential benefits of community-owned energy schemes, including renewable power generation and efficient energy distribution networks. These initiatives are designed not only to provide cleaner, more affordable energy options but also to foster greater community engagement and cohesion around shared environmental goals.
