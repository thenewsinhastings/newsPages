In a significant push for local sustainability and energy efficiency, several groups in Hastings and Rye have joined forces to advocate for greater investment in community energy projects. The initiative seeks to unlock the potential of collective energy generation and management across both towns.

Organisers believe that by working together, residents can significantly reduce their carbon footprint while also achieving substantial cost savings on utility bills. A series of public meetings are planned to engage more members of the community and gather input for future projects.

Representatives from the Hastings Community Energy Group (HCEG) stated: “We’re excited about the possibilities that come with collaborative efforts in energy management. Our goal is to build a sustainable framework that benefits all residents.”

Similar sentiments were echoed by Rye’s Environmental Sustainability Committee, who see this as an opportunity for both towns to lead by example in environmental stewardship.

The community response has been overwhelmingly positive so far, with many individuals and small businesses expressing interest in participating. The groups are now seeking partnerships with local authorities and energy providers to advance their plans further.

For more information or to get involved, interested parties can contact the Hastings Community Energy Group at info@hceg.org.uk.
