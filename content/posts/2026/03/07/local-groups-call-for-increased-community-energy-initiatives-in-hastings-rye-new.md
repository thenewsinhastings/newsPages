HASTINGS AND RYE — A coalition of local groups and community leaders has urged the government to support initiatives aimed at unlocking greater potential for community-led renewable energy projects across Hastings and Rye. This call comes as part of a broader national movement advocating for more accessible and scalable community energy solutions.

The group, comprising representatives from various environmental NGOs, local businesses, and residents' associations, convened this week to discuss the challenges and opportunities presented by current regulations and funding structures. They highlighted that while there is significant interest in developing renewable energy projects within the community, barriers such as high upfront costs and complex regulatory frameworks have hindered progress.

Participants also emphasized the need for greater collaboration between local authorities, private enterprises, and grassroots movements to ensure that any new initiatives are inclusive and beneficial to all members of the community. The meeting concluded with a pledge from participants to continue advocating for legislative changes that would ease restrictions on small-scale renewable projects and incentivize investment in sustainable energy solutions.

For more information or to get involved in local efforts, interested parties can contact the Hastings & Rye Community Energy Group via their official website.
