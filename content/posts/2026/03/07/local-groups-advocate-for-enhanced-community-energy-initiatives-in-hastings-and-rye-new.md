Residents and community leaders from Hastings and Rye are rallying behind a call to 'unlock community energy at scale', aiming to increase local involvement in renewable energy projects. The initiative seeks to harness the collective power of residents and businesses to fund, develop, and own renewable energy installations that benefit the entire community.

The campaign has gained significant traction among environmental groups, local councils, and grassroots movements advocating for sustainable practices. Supporters believe that by empowering communities with control over their energy sources, there will be a greater sense of ownership and responsibility towards reducing carbon footprints and promoting clean energy solutions.

Representatives from various organizations have expressed support for the proposal, stressing its potential to create jobs locally while fostering a more resilient and sustainable future. The initiative aims to facilitate dialogue between community members, policymakers, and industry experts in order to develop practical steps toward achieving these goals.
