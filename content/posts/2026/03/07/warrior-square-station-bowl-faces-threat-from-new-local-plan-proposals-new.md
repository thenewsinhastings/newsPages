Residents of Hastings are concerned over recent proposals in the new local plan that could affect the Warrior Square Station Bowl, a cherished community landmark. The proposed changes have sparked debate and worry among locals who value the historical significance and recreational use of the area.

The latest draft from the Hastings Council has outlined potential modifications to the bowl, which could lead to alterations that detract from its current character. Community groups are now urging for public consultations where residents can voice their opinions on how the bowl should be preserved and developed in alignment with modern needs while respecting historical integrity.

Sarah Jenkins, Chief Reporter at our local newspaper, said: 'The Station Bowl holds a special place in many hearts, and it's crucial that its future is decided through community engagement and careful planning.' She added, 'We're calling on all residents to get involved and participate actively in the upcoming consultation processes.'

For more information on how to contribute your views or attend public meetings regarding this proposal, visit the Hastings Council website.
