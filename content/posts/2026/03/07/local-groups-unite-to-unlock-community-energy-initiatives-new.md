Residents and organizations in Hastings and Rye are rallying behind a new initiative aimed at unlocking community energy projects on a larger scale. The move is part of an effort to increase local engagement with renewable energy sources, promoting sustainability within the region.

The coalition comprises various local groups including environmental charities, community associations, and educational institutions. They believe that by working together, they can create more substantial impacts in reducing carbon footprints while also fostering economic growth for small-scale initiatives.

Organizers of the campaign assert that access to funding, technological resources, and policy support are crucial elements needed to facilitate these projects. They hope to engage with local councils and businesses to secure additional backing from both governmental bodies and private sectors alike.

As part of their outreach efforts, several town hall meetings have been scheduled in the coming weeks to discuss potential collaboration opportunities and share ideas on how best to implement such schemes effectively throughout Hastings and Rye.
