In a recent development aimed at fostering local sustainability, several community groups in Hastings and Rye have rallied behind the call to 'unlock community energy at scale'. This initiative seeks to empower residents by enabling them to generate their own renewable energy through collective projects.

The push for increased community involvement in energy production comes as part of broader efforts to reduce carbon footprints and promote green living. Local advocates believe that such initiatives can not only enhance environmental sustainability but also contribute positively to the local economy by creating jobs and reducing energy costs for households.

Community leaders are urging both Hastings Borough Council and Rye Town Council to provide support through regulatory adjustments and financial incentives, making it easier for communities to establish small-scale renewable energy projects. The ultimate goal is to create a model that can be replicated across other areas within the region, demonstrating how collective action on sustainability can yield significant benefits.

As part of this initiative, several workshops and public meetings are planned in both towns over the coming weeks, aiming to inform residents about the possibilities and engage them in shaping these new energy projects. Local groups hope that these events will serve as a catalyst for broader community involvement and collaboration towards achieving a more sustainable future.
