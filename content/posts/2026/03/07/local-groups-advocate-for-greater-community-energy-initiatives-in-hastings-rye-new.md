In a recent development aimed at enhancing local sustainability and community engagement, various groups within Hastings and Rye have united to advocate for the 'unlocking of community energy at scale'. This initiative seeks to empower residents by facilitating greater access to renewable energy sources and promoting self-sufficiency in energy production.

Supporters of this campaign believe that by fostering a more decentralized approach to energy generation, local communities can not only reduce their carbon footprint but also enjoy economic benefits through the creation of green jobs. The proposed measures include the installation of solar panels on public buildings and community-owned wind turbines.

Several local organisations including environmental activists from ‘Green Hastings’ and representatives from the Rye Town Council have been at the forefront of this movement, urging both government bodies and private investors to support such projects. They argue that with the right policies in place, these initiatives could serve as models for other towns looking to increase their reliance on renewable energy.

The campaign has gained significant traction among residents who are increasingly concerned about climate change and seek practical solutions within their communities. As discussions continue, there is a growing optimism that Hastings and Rye will lead the way in demonstrating how community-driven projects can play a crucial role in national efforts towards sustainability.
