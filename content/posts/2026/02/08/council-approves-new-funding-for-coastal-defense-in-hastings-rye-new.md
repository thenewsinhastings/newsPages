The Hastings Borough Council and the Rother District Council have approved significant funding to enhance coastal defense measures in Hastings and Rye. The decision, which comes after months of discussions and consultations with local communities, aims to protect both towns from rising sea levels and severe weather conditions.

Council leaders emphasized that the investment will not only safeguard homes and businesses but also support the long-term sustainability of these historic seaside areas. The funds will be used for reinforcing existing seawalls, upgrading drainage systems, and implementing early warning technology to alert residents during emergencies.

Representatives from both councils highlighted the importance of collaboration between local authorities, central government departments, and community groups in addressing climate change impacts on coastal communities. Public consultations are set to continue as part of an ongoing effort to involve all stakeholders in decision-making processes regarding future infrastructure projects.

Local businesses and residents have shown support for these initiatives, recognizing their vital role in maintaining the economic vitality and quality of life in Hastings and Rye.
