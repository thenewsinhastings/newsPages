The annual Hastings Pier Festival returned to the iconic pier this weekend, celebrating local culture and heritage with a host of events for all ages. The festival kicked off on Saturday morning with an opening ceremony that included speeches from local dignitaries and cultural figures. Throughout the day, visitors were treated to live music performances, artisan craft stalls showcasing handmade goods, and traditional Hastings food and drink stands.

A highlight of the event was a heritage walk led by the Hastings Historical Society, which provided insights into the town’s rich maritime history. Additionally, children enjoyed face painting and storytelling sessions in the dedicated kids’ zone.

Organisers were pleased with the turnout, estimating that over 5000 people attended across the weekend. Local businesses also benefitted from the influx of visitors, with many reporting increased sales throughout the event period.

The Hastings Pier Festival is an integral part of the community calendar and continues to attract a diverse range of participants each year.
