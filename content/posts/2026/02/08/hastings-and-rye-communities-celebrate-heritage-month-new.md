Local residents in Hastings and Rye have been enthusiastically participating in the annual Heritage Month celebrations. This year's event includes a variety of activities such as guided historical tours, art exhibitions featuring local artists inspired by the town’s rich history, and live storytelling sessions at the historic Rye Castle Museum.

Mayor Annabelle Smith encouraged all community members to take part in these enriching events, stating that "Heritage Month is an excellent opportunity for us to come together as a community and appreciate our unique cultural heritage. It’s important we continue to support and promote local initiatives such as these." 

The festival aims not only to entertain but also to educate the younger generation about their roots and encourage them to explore the town's historical sites, fostering a deeper connection with the area’s past.

Organizers are optimistic that this year's Heritage Month will set new records in terms of attendance and engagement. The month-long celebration is packed with activities planned for every weekend until October 31st.
