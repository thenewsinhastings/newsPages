The historic town of Rye celebrated its centenary this weekend with a series of events that drew hundreds of residents and visitors alike. The festivities, which included a procession through the town’s ancient streets, were organized by local volunteers to commemorate the community's rich history and cultural heritage.

Mayor Caroline Thompson praised the efforts of all involved in making the event such a success. 'This is a fantastic opportunity for our community to come together and reflect on Rye's past while looking towards its future,' she said during an opening ceremony at Mermaid Theatre, where local schoolchildren performed traditional songs and dances.

The celebrations also featured a variety of stalls selling crafts and locally produced goods, showcasing the town’s vibrant creative industries. Local historians provided guided tours around key historical sites, allowing visitors to learn more about Rye's unique architectural features and its importance as a strategic port during medieval times.
