The town of Rye marked its centenary this week with a series of events and festivities that attracted thousands of visitors. The celebrations kicked off on Saturday morning with a parade through the historic streets, followed by live music performances at various venues throughout the day.

Local residents were encouraged to participate in traditional crafts workshops and historical reenactments, showcasing Rye’s rich heritage and cultural significance. Mayor Jane Thompson praised the community's effort in organizing such an eventful weekend and thanked all volunteers who helped make it possible.

'Our town has a long history of coming together for important occasions,' said Thompson during her speech at the main stage. 'This year, as we celebrate our centenary, it is heartwarming to see how much support there has been from both locals and visitors alike.'

The weekend also featured a food festival showcasing local culinary delights and stalls selling unique Rye crafts and souvenirs. Proceeds from these events will go towards supporting the town’s community projects.

As the festivities concluded, many expressed hope that such unity would continue to be a hallmark of life in Hastings & Rye.
