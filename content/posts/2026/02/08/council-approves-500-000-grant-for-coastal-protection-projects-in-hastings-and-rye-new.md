The Hastings Borough Council and the Rother District Council have approved a joint grant of £500,000 aimed at bolstering coastal protection measures in both Hastings and Rye. The funding will be used to support ongoing projects that aim to protect local communities from rising sea levels and severe weather events.

Speaking on behalf of the councils, Councillor Jane Smith emphasized the importance of the investment, stating, “This is a significant step forward for our coastal resilience strategy. We are committed to safeguarding both Hastings and Rye against future environmental challenges.”

The announcement follows months of negotiations between local authorities and government bodies, highlighting the collaborative effort required to address these pressing issues.

Residents in both towns have expressed their relief at this news, recognising the potential impact on coastal erosion and flooding risks. Community leaders are optimistic that these measures will provide long-term benefits for residents and businesses alike.

For more information on specific projects being funded by this grant, interested parties can contact their local council offices or visit the respective websites of Hastings Borough Council and Rother District Council.
