In a significant move to protect the coastal town of Hastings against rising sea levels and increased storm activity, the local council has approved funding for essential flood defence measures. The budget allocation is part of a larger initiative to safeguard the region's heritage and infrastructure from potential climate change impacts.

The project includes the installation of new barriers along key stretches of the coast as well as improvements to drainage systems in low-lying areas of Rye. This development comes after months of consultation with local communities, environmental experts, and government agencies.

Councillor Sarah Green, who spearheaded the proposal, highlighted the importance of proactive measures: 'We are acutely aware of the risks faced by our coastline and it is crucial that we act now to protect both our residents and businesses.'

The budget also includes provisions for ongoing maintenance and monitoring of these defences, ensuring their effectiveness over time. Environmental advocates have praised this decision as a step in the right direction towards sustainable coastal management.
