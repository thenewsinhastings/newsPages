HASTINGS & RYE — In a vibrant display of community spirit, Hastings and Rye celebrated their rich heritage this week with an array of engaging events and activities. From historical tours to cultural workshops, the programme offered something for everyone.

The opening day kicked off in Hastings with the 'Streets Through Time' event, where costumed performers brought the town’s history to life. Local historians shared fascinating stories from the past, attracting visitors and locals alike who were eager to learn more about their community's roots.

In Rye, residents gathered for a traditional market day showcasing local crafts and produce alongside demonstrations of old-world skills such as spinning and weaving. The market was bustling with excitement as families explored stalls filled with unique items and delightful treats.

Throughout the week, educational workshops were held to teach children about their town’s heritage through interactive activities. These included storytelling sessions, guided tours around historic buildings, and craft-making workshops inspired by ancient traditions.

The Heritage Week concluded on Sunday with a grand parade in Hastings, featuring colourful floats representing different eras of local history. Participants wore costumes reflecting the style and culture of various periods, adding to the festive atmosphere. The parade was followed by a community picnic where everyone could share stories and enjoy each other’s company.

Organisers praised the turnout and enthusiasm from all those involved in making Heritage Week such a memorable event for both residents and visitors. They expressed hope that this celebration would help foster a greater appreciation of Hastings’ and Rye’s rich cultural heritage and encourage more community engagement year-round.
