The annual Hastings and Rye Festival took place over the weekend, bringing together residents and visitors alike for a celebration of local culture, art, music, and heritage. The event featured a variety of activities including live performances, food stalls, children's games, and historical reenactments that showcased the rich history of both towns.

Organisers reported high attendance figures, with over 20,000 people visiting the festival grounds across the two-day event. Local businesses also saw a boost in trade, particularly those located along the High Street where many food vendors and craft stalls were situated.

The festival is an important part of the community calendar and provides a platform for local artists and performers to showcase their talents on a wider stage. This year's theme was 'Celebrating Our Heritage', which saw numerous exhibits highlighting the historical significance of Hastings and Rye, from ancient castles to modern-day landmarks.
