The Hastings Borough Council has entered discussions on the proposed new climate action plan aimed at reducing carbon emissions and promoting sustainability within the borough. The initiative includes plans to increase green spaces, improve public transport options, and encourage local businesses to adopt more environmentally friendly practices.

During the council meeting held yesterday, members debated various aspects of the proposal, with some councillors expressing concern over potential economic impacts while others emphasized the long-term benefits for both the environment and local residents. The plan is expected to go before a full council vote in the coming weeks.

Community groups and environmental activists have shown strong support for the initiative, believing it will help Hastings lead the way in addressing climate change at a local level. However, local business owners have raised concerns about potential costs and regulations that could impact their operations.
