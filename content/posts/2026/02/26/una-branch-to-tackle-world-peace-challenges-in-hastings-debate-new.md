The United Nations Association (UNA) branch in Hastings is set to hold a critical debate this week, focusing on the key issues that are currently threatening global peace and stability. The event aims to engage members of the community in an informed discussion about the challenges facing international relations today.

Speakers from various backgrounds will contribute their expertise, offering unique insights into topics such as conflict resolution, human rights violations, and environmental sustainability - all crucial factors influencing world peace. The debate is scheduled for this Thursday evening at the Hastings Town Hall and promises to be an enlightening event for anyone interested in understanding how these global issues impact local communities.

Organisers hope that by bringing together diverse perspectives, attendees will gain a deeper appreciation of the complexities involved in maintaining international peace and security. The UNA Hastings branch encourages all members of the public to attend this free-to-enter debate, which is open to everyone regardless of membership status.
